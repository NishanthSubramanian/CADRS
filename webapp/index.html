  • CADRS


    (Coordinated & Autonomous
    Drone Rescue System)

  • Challenges

    CADRS (Coordinated & Autonomous Drone Rescue System) is a drone rescue system in which drones are deployed in a specified area and the drones find the best possible path to deliver various payloads like first aid kits, food and clothing to locations that are not easily accessible. They may also be used to deliver a large number of items in a short span of time.

    • Challenge 1: To get the shortest path from the drone(source) to all the areas of delivery.
    • Challenge 2: To be able to move the drones efficiently across the area without collisions and clashes.
    • Challenge 3: To get the system of drones to operate in terms of the urgency of the rescue operation.
    • Challenge 3: To be able to establish a connection between the drones and a common ground control.

  • Working

    The objective of our drone system is to enable a central server detect urgency calls from distinct areas. This centralized server controls a system of drones.

    • There are known emergency points where people are in need of supplies, called nodes that the drones have to deliver.
    • Drones have cameras on them to enable computer vision into these drones.
    • During their operation of delivering to the known nodes, it may come across new nodes (new people that the drone just recognized during its operation and who need the drone to stop and drop off a few supplies).
    • The Rescue operation is a time-critical application where continuous connectivity to ground personnel is mandatory. Thus, persistent network connectivity is desirable to propagate information efﬁciently. Therfore, Wi-Fi cards have been employed in the drone.
    • Since there's no size constraint on our centralized server, we can employ a Wi-Fi card lke IEEE 802.11
    • For example, IEEE 802.11 could be employed as it is easier to transmit contant information in a short period of time

  • Algorithm

    All the known nodes have a quantified attribute called urgency assigned to them (1-100). Number of drones is d and number of nodes is n. Cost to travel from every node to every other is first calculated. Cost of travelling between two nodes depends on two attributes: distance between them and urgency of the destination node. Let the area under consideration be A. The server controls ‘d’ drones .A is divided into d regions, each region assigned to a drone. In each region, the number of nodes may vary. But weight of the minimum spanning tree in every region as small as possible and as close to each other as possible to achieve maximum efficiency in drone movement. This weight of a minimum spanning tree of any region is addition of the cost of traversing all the nodes of that region via the least costly path.
